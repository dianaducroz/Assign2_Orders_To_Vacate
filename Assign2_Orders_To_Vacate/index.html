Orders to Vacate in New York City

Number of Housing Units Ordered to be Vacated, 2012 to 2017

The number of housing units ordered vacated for safety reasons by the NYC Department of Housing Preservation & Development increased by 14.9% across the city for the 5-year period from 2012–2017.

Orders to vacate are issued by the city for three main causes: fire damage, habitability issues (unhealthy or unsafe conditions), and illegal occupancy (unpermitted units).

Citywide, fire damage was by far the top reason for ‘vacate’ orders – approximately two-thirds of all cases - followed by illegal occupancy. Illegal occupancy violations had the greatest percentage change over the period, with a growth of 43.7% citywide.

Brooklyn and Queens, the two boroughs with the most units vacated because of illegal occupancy, registered increases of 35.5% and 37.6% respectively over the period.

Public relocation assistance is often available to tenants wno are displaced by vacate orders, but residents displaced from illegal units are ineligible for these benefits.

Queens had the largest percentage increase in the number of units ordered vacated for all causes, from 138 units in 2012 to 265 units in 2017, a 92% percent change. The Bronx had the second highest percentage increase at 78.4% (264 units in 2012 to 471 units in 2017).

Brooklyn, on the other hand, recorded a 21.4% decrease between 2012 and 2017, from 505 units in 2012 to 397 in 2017, despite having the largest overall number of units affected of all five boroughs over the time period.
